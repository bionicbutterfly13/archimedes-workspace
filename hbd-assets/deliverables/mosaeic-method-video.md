## Marketing Copy (Model Format)

**Deliverable:** The MoSAEIC Method (Video from the Hidden Block Decoder)

**Feature:** A real-time awareness tool designed specifically for analytical empaths. MoSAEIC stands for Mental Observation of Sensations, Actions, Emotions, Impulses, and Cognitions. Provides a systematic sanity check when overwhelm hits — breaking down the onslaught into observable, manageable parts.

**Functional Benefit:** You gain the ability to do a quick system check in the moment. Instead of drowning in undifferentiated overwhelm, you can identify which channel is flooded and which is driving the spiral. Creates space between experience and reaction.

**Experiential Benefit:** You're in a meeting and feel the familiar freeze coming on. Instead of white-knuckling through it, you run a quick MoSAEIC scan: sensations (jaw tight), emotions (fear of being wrong), cognitions ("they'll see I don't belong"). You've located the source. The overwhelm becomes data. You respond instead of react.

**Emotional Benefit:** You feel a sense of control that doesn't require suppression. You're not fighting your system — you're reading it. The chaos becomes navigable because you finally have a dashboard.

- **I**mpulses
- **C**ognitions

## The Five Domains of Observation

### S — Sensations
Your five senses: sight, hearing, taste, touch, smell.

**What to notice:**
- Is there tension in your shoulders?
- Tightness in your chest?
- A sense of unease?
- Heightened awareness of particular scents (common in PTSD)?

**The question:** What's the incoming sensory information? What are you feeling physically?

---

### A — Actions
What you're doing or avoiding doing right now.

**What to notice:**
- Are you fidgeting?
- Avoiding eye contact?
- Checking your phone as a tension break?

**Key insight:** Don't try to stop the action — actions have momentum. By the time you're taking action, you're already moving. The goal is to notice when you find yourself doing something that doesn't contribute to your desired outcome. Non-judgmentally observe.

---

### E — Emotions
The feeling tones underneath your thoughts.

**What to notice:**
- Are you frustrated? Anxious? Excited but nervous?
- Angry? Sad?
- Is the emotion fleeting or are you stuck in it?

**The dynamics:** Where does the emotion take you next? Does it drive you to action? Does it bring attention back to sensations? Does it go back and forth, making a mess even as you're taking action?

---

### I — Impulses
Urges and desires pulling at you BEFORE they become actions.

**What to notice:**
- The urge to interrupt someone
- The urge to leave a situation
- The impulse to prove yourself right

**Key insight:** Impulses are often blended with emotions — they may be your system's attempt to manage the emotions. Before an impulse becomes an action is the best time to intervene. Not to suppress, but to process.

**Processing an impulse:**
1. Realize its intensity
2. Realize what it suggests you do
3. Identify the emotions behind it
4. Ask: What can I do to dull the impulse? (Breathe? Walk away? Visualize consequences?)

---

### C — Cognitions
Thoughts, inner dialogues, mental stories, things you imagine.

**What to notice:**
- "This isn't working"
- "I should know better"
- "They don't show me respect"

**The trap:** As a result of thinking these thoughts, you may find yourself believing them to be true. If they're true, then "what am I going to do about it?" → moves to impulses charged with emotions → emotions cascade to impulses → spiral.

## The Foundation: Goldman's Breathing Framework

MoSAEIC is built on Daniel Goldman's breathing framework as the core.

**Why this foundation:**
- Builds your focusing muscle
- Trains your ability to stay grounded
- Develops the "noticing" skill — where the magic happens

**The three networks being trained:**

1. **Default Mode Network** — The self-referential storytelling network
2. **Salience Network** — Realizes when something has pulled your attention away
3. **Prefrontal/Parietal Circuits** — The executive function that brings attention back (requires cognitive effort)

---

## Practice Instructions

### Step 1: Find a comfortable position
Sitting or standing (lying down risks falling asleep)

### Step 2: Hands on abdomen
Watching hands rise and fall serves as proxy for breath awareness

### Step 3: Notice when mind wanders
From observing the hand rising and falling

### Step 4: Bring it back
That's the training — the return

**What to notice during practice:**
- The energy it takes to return attention
- How emotions, thoughts, and other aspects fight you returning to focus
- All the different things that pull attention in different places

## The Power of This System

**Works on the go.** You don't have to lock yourself away, sit crisscross applesauce, and hum. 

**Use pressure situations AS the practice.** Even if at the last minute you're in a situation that makes you wish you had meditated — that situation becomes the opportunity.

**Scales with intensity.** With increasing practice, you can bring resources to bear on tougher and tougher situations.

**Moves you from overwhelm to control.** By breaking situations into pieces, you go from drowning to directing.
