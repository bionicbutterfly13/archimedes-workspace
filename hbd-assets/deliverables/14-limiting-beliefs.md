## Belief #1: I Don't Have Enough Time

**Limiting Belief:** There isn't enough time in the day to do what I want and need to do.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- I have time for my most important tasks
- My schedule gives me freedom from time pressure
- I don't let time rule my life
- I get things done without worrying about time
- I'm productive and make the best out of the time I have been given

**Action:** Work on the thing that matters most FIRST thing in the day ("slay your dragons first")

**Affirmation:** "There's more than enough time in each day for me to accomplish the tasks that matter most. I'm dedicated to and focused on getting the most important thing done each day. I refuse to be easily distracted. Instead, I slay my dragons first thing each morning."

---

## Belief #2: I'm Too Old to Start Something New

**Limiting Belief:** Only young people can start new things. I'm too old to reset my career, start a hobby, or pursue new goals.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- My age gives me wisdom
- I have more experience than those who are younger
- I can avoid mistakes that younger people make
- My experience will allow me to get going faster
- I'm never too old to start something new

**Proof:** Joy Behar (40), Vera Wang (40), Harlan Sanders (65), Charles Darwin (50)

**Affirmation:** "I can start something new at any age. In fact, my age gives me a distinct advantage over those who are younger. I relish the experience and wisdom that age has provided me."

---

## Belief #3: Past Failure Means Future Failure

**Limiting Belief:** I always fail at this, so why should this time be any different?

**Empowering Replacement:**
- My past failure has no bearing on my future
- Past failures help me avoid future mistakes
- I've simply discovered a way that doesn't work
- Every failure brings me one step closer to success

**Wisdom:** Thomas Edison — "I have not failed. I've just found 10,000 ways that won't work."

**Affirmation:** "I avoid thinking that my past failures could ever cause me to fail now or in the future. I affirm that each past failure has actually brought me one step closer to success."

---

## Belief #4: The Past Is Likely to Influence the Future

**Limiting Belief:** My past actions hold too much sway over my future actions. My mistakes will keep haunting me.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- My past can't determine what I achieve in the future
- My past is one of my greatest assets
- Lessons from the past make me wiser in the future
- I can avoid mistakes in the future by looking to the past
- I can learn from my past, adapt, and change

**Key Insight:** Your past influences the future in GOOD ways — more knowledge, skills, experience.

**Affirmation:** "I affirm that my past fails to determine my future. In fact, it gives me great advantages. I alone determine my future. I adapt, change, and evolve in positive ways because of my past."

---

## Belief #5: My Resources Are Limited

**Limiting Belief:** There aren't enough resources for me to do what I want. I don't have enough time/money/connections.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- The universe is full of abundant resources
- There's more than enough to go around
- I can get whatever I need to accomplish my wildest dreams
- The universe has my back
- I create more of what I focus on

**Mindset Shift:** From scarcity mindset → abundance mindset

**Affirmation:** "The universe is abundant and has my back in every way. I believe there's more than enough for me and that resources are plentiful. Everything I need to achieve my goals comes my way when I need it."

---

## Belief #6: Lack of Major Progress Means Failure

**Limiting Belief:** If I'm not making significant progress every single day, then I'm a failure.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- Small progress equals a big win
- Small successes add up to big progress over time
- I celebrate my victories no matter what size they are
- Consistency is what matters most

**Key Insight:** Judge success by CONSISTENCY, not by size of daily progress.

**Affirmation:** "I affirm that I am making progress on my goals even if that progress may seem small and relatively insignificant. I choose to celebrate my small wins knowing that each small win brings me one step closer to my overall goal."

---

## Belief #7: I Compare Myself to Others

**Limiting Belief:** I should be accomplishing at least as much, if not more, than others.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- The achievements of others don't determine my value
- I am valuable simply because of who I am
- I refuse to compare myself to others
- What matters most is what I achieve, not what others achieve
- I am more than my accomplishments

**Wisdom:** Theodore Roosevelt — "Comparison is the thief of joy."

**Affirmation:** "I refuse to believe that my value is tied to my achievements. I know that I am valuable and worthy simply because of who I am. The achievements of others have no bearing on my value."

---

## Belief #8: I'm Not Responsible for My Current Situation

**Limiting Belief:** Someone else is responsible for where I am. I'm a victim of circumstances.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- I am 100% responsible for my life
- I take ownership of all my circumstances
- I refuse to play the victim game
- I can create whatever circumstances I want
- I'm in complete charge of my life and determine what it looks like

**Key Insight:** Once you take ownership, you can change it. Playing victim = staying stuck.

**Affirmation:** "I am the captain of my fate. I am responsible for everything good and bad that comes into my life. I take 100% ownership for the current state of my life. I do what is necessary to create the life I desire."

---

## Belief #9: I Don't Deserve Success

**Limiting Belief:** I am not good enough, smart enough, or lovable enough to be worthy of success.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- I am inherently worthy of success
- I am valuable simply because of who I am
- I deserve success and all the benefits that come from it

**Key Insight:** You don't have to DO anything to be worthy. You are worthy because you are you.

**Affirmation:** "I affirm that I am worthy of all the success in the world. Regardless of what anyone has said to me, regardless of what I have thought in the past, I choose now to believe that I am worthy of success."

---

## Belief #10: I Worry What Other People Think of Me

**Limiting Belief:** If I do or don't do certain actions, others will think less of me.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- What others think of me doesn't matter
- What truly matters is what I think of myself
- My opinion of myself is the one that counts
- I am secure in who I am
- I don't strive to make everyone happy

**Affirmation:** "I refuse to play the people pleasing game. I affirm that the opinions of others fail to have any sway over me. My opinion of myself is all that matters."

---

## Belief #11: I Don't Give Myself the Love I Give to Others

**Limiting Belief:** I should be perfect. If I'm not perfect, I need to punish myself.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- I'm human and it's okay to make mistakes
- I deserve the same grace, compassion, and understanding I give to others
- I need to be patient and loving towards myself
- I should treat myself as kindly as I treat others

**Key Question:** "How would I treat someone else who was in my shoes?"

**Affirmation:** "I'm human, just like everyone else. It's okay for me to struggle and make mistakes. When that happens, I deserve the same compassion and grace I give to others."

---

## Belief #12: I Can Do Everything Myself

**Limiting Belief:** Asking for help is a sign of weakness. I need to do everything alone.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- I can benefit from the help and assistance of others
- Others have strengths and talents that I don't have
- I can't reach my full potential without the help of others
- I can't do everything by myself

**Key Insight:** NOT being able to ask for help is the real sign of weakness.

**Affirmation:** "Although I'm skilled, talented, and capable, I affirm that I need the help and assistance of others. They supplement my talent so that together we can excel at any task."

---

## Belief #13: I'm Not Smart Enough

**Limiting Belief:** I don't have the necessary intelligence to accomplish what I truly want.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- I've accomplished so much already in my life
- If I wasn't smart, I wouldn't have gotten this far
- I have an incredible ability to learn
- I can do anything I set my mind to

**Proof:** Look at everything you've already learned and accomplished.

**Affirmation:** "I am highly intelligent and accomplished. I have already achieved many things in my life which proves my intelligence. If I don't know how to do something, I'm able to quickly learn it."

---

## Belief #14: I'm Not Ready to Start

**Limiting Belief:** I don't have enough pieces in place to effectively get started.

**Empowering Replacement:**
- Progress matters more than perfection
- I can always make changes as I go
- If I wait until everything is perfect, I'll never get started
- I should start sooner rather than later
- I can't wait around for my dreams to come true

**Wisdom:** "Ready, fire, aim" — correct course as you go.

**Affirmation:** "I believe in getting started now. I avoid waiting until all things are perfect in order to get started with my idea. I do my best to get things in order, but I refuse to believe the lie that everything can be perfect."
