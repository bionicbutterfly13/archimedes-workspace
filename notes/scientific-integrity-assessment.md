## Key Limitations to Acknowledge

### 1. Small Effect Sizes
DMN differences in ADHD are statistically significant but small (d=0.14-0.17), meaning "subtle group differences". This doesn't invalidate the findings but suggests the **"meat cleaver" is more metaphorical than literal**.

### 2. "Analytical Empath with ADHD" Not a Researched Subtype
While ADHD, HSP, and 2e are all established, their **intersection as a distinct clinical phenotype is observational, not validated taxonomy**.

### 3. Proprietary Components Lack Outcome Data
IAS needs pilot data showing client outcomes (symptom reduction, skill improvement, satisfaction).

### 4. Pedagogical Frameworks May Be Mistaken for Neuroscience
Terms like "Filter layer" and "Five Pillars" are **teaching tools, not technical neuroscience terms**.

## What Would Strengthen It

1. **Clearer labeling:** Distinguish "established neuroscience" from "clinical synthesis" from "pedagogical framework"
2. **Pilot outcome data:** Even N=10-20 case studies for IAS would add credibility
3. **Acknowledge effect sizes:** Small effects still matter clinically, but transparency builds trust
4. **Frame "Analytical Empath with ADHD":** As a "clinical phenotype hypothesis" rather than validated subtype
