# How To: Restore an External Recovery Signal Early

Deploy an **external exoskeleton** that functions as a surrogate executive system. This is not about "thinking harder"—it's about placing decision-making structures **outside** your mind when internal regulation is offline.

---

## 1. Offload the "Internal Governor" to an Exoskeleton

You cannot rely on internal willpower during a "shame spiral" or cognitive overload. You must externalize the next steps so they remain accessible when your "Filter" layer fails.

**Mechanism:** Create a physical or digital "surrogate filter" that holds your decision-making and next-step pathways outside the self.

**How to do it:**
- Establish a permanent external structure (visual workflow, checklist, "next-step" card) at your physical workspace
- When mental fog hits, **do not ask "What should I do?"**—obey the external prompt
- This maintains psychological "traction" even when internal focus is flooded

---

## 2. Make the "Pathway Back to Traction" Visible

The ADHD brain enters the "Replay-Abandon-Restart" cycle because it predicts the current error is unfixable. You must **visually contradict** this prediction.

**Mechanism:** The external structure provides a visible recovery pathway that stabilizes chaotic internal predictions by aligning them with stable sensory inputs.

**How to do it:**
- Keep a visual map of the project showing where you are and exactly how to re-enter the workflow after disruption
- By **seeing** the path, you prevent the brain's false prediction of failure from "locking in"
- Allows you to stay in the context rather than abandoning for a "fresh start"

---

## 3. Deploy the "Vital Pause" (MOSAEIC Protocol)

Catch signal loss before it becomes total paralysis. Use MOSAEIC to detect which specific "window" of awareness is causing the drift.

**The 5 Windows:**
- **S**ensations
- **A**ctions
- **E**motions
- **I**mpulses
- **C**ognitions

**How to do it:**
- When you feel the urge to check out (Impulse window) or feel tightness in your chest (Sensation window), pause
- Name the specific window capturing your attention
- This creates a "cognitive wedge" separating you from the impulse

**Why it works:**
- Prevents "falling into the trap of intellectualization" or defensive reacting
- Turns catastrophic feeling ("I'm failing") into data point ("I am having the sensation of tightness")
- Reduces amygdala response

---

## 4. Activate a "Somatic Anchor" at the Point of Performance

Because the "meat cleaver" effect disconnects intention from action, you cannot **think** your way back into movement; you must **move** your way back.

**Mechanism:** Use a Somatic Anchor or Ignition Switch at the exact moment action is required (the "point of performance").

**How to do it:**
- Implement a "micro-yes" protocol
- A tiny, rhythmic physical movement: tapping, standing up, specific breath pattern
- Bypasses the frozen frontal lobe

**Why it works:**
- Acts as a "literal synaptic bridge"
- Generates enough neural energy to initiate action
- Does NOT trigger the nervous system's threat response

---

## 5. Run "Mismatch Experiments" (The Conviction Gauntlet)

To **permanently** restore the signal, you must update the brain's belief that "mistakes = non-recoverability."

**Mechanism:** Engage in Mismatch Experiments where you intentionally take imperfect action to trigger fear, then observe that the catastrophe does not occur.

**How to do it:**
1. When the urge to abandon arises, force a small, **imperfect** continuation of the task
2. When the predicted disaster fails to materialize, the brain registers a "prediction error"
3. Brain updates its model: "recovery IS possible"

---

## The Core Principle

By externalizing these functions, you **stop asking a dysregulated brain to regulate itself**.

You provide the "environmental predictability" required to stabilize your internal models, ensuring that your identity and actions remain synchronized even under load.
